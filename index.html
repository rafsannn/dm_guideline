    📊 Phase 1: Question Pattern Analysis

    A data-driven breakdown of the Midterm structure. Based on Spring 2023 papers, this identifies the highest ROI topics for your time.

    Weight Distribution Historical

    Calculations and logic evaluations carry the most weight in past papers.

    Core Exam Observations

    • High Probability: Truth tables for compound propositions and counting rules ($P$ & $C$) consistently appear as 5+ mark questions.
    • Direct Marks: Proving $A \times B \neq B \times A$ and differentiating One-to-One/Onto are standard Part 'b' questions.
    • Group Strategy: Answer 2 out of 3. Typically, Group A focuses on Ch 1 & 2, while Group B hits Ch 3 hard.

    ⚠️ Preparation Gap Alert

    Past papers include the Pigeonhole Principle and the Permutation Proof $P(n,r) = C(n,r) \cdot P(r,r)$. Study these from the textbook sections as they are missing from some slide summaries.

          The Quick Wins

          1

          Truth Tables (5.5 Marks)

          Mechanical marks. Master the implication ($\rightarrow$). $(p \rightarrow q) \vee (\neg p \rightarrow q)$ is a recurring favorite.

          2

          Set Equality Proofs

          Proving $A \times B \neq B \times A$. Define your sets $A=\{a,b\}$ and $B=\{1,2\}$ and show specific mismatched pairs like $(a, 1) \neq (1, a)$.

          3

          Double Summation

          Solve $\sum \sum i$ by evaluating the inner loop first. It's essentially simple arithmetic.

          Critical Logic Translations

          "p only if q" $p \rightarrow q$
          "p is necessary for q" $q \rightarrow p$
          "neither p nor q" $\neg p \wedge \neg q$
          "p is sufficient for q" $p \rightarrow q$
